Discovering Kerala: Unveiling the Charms of God's Own Country

Kerala, a state on India's tropical Malabar Coast, has nearly 600km of Arabian Sea shoreline.It is known for its lush greenery, beautiful backwaters, and unique cultural heritage. Overall, Kerala is celebrated for its natural beauty, cultural richness, progressive outlook, and unique experiences, earning it the title of "God's Own Country."

Backwaters

Kerala is renowned for its tranquil backwaters, a network of interconnected canals, rivers, lakes, and lagoons. Visitors often explore these backwaters on traditional houseboats, providing a unique and serene experience. One can cruise down the backwaters while enjoying the unique feel of the “Kettuvallams” or house boats which provide amenities including a taste of typical Kerala cuisine.The backwaters have a unique ecosystem: freshwater from the rivers meets the seawater from the Arabian Sea.

Ayurveda

Kerala is a hub for traditional Ayurvedic treatments and therapies. Many tourists visit Kerala to experience authentic Ayurvedic massages, rejuvenation therapies, and wellness treatments.Ayurveda of Kerala is quite popular because of the five prolonged treatment which is termed as panchakarma. This form of treatment includes the use of medicated oils, milk, Ayurvedic herbs along with a special diet for the purpose of treating various types of health issues as well as illnesses.

Culture and Arts

Kerala boasts a rich cultural heritage, with vibrant art forms such as Kathakali (classical dance-drama), Mohiniyattam (classical dance form), Theyyam (ritualistic performance art), and traditional music like Carnatic music. The state also celebrates numerous festivals with great fervor, including Onam, the harvest festival. In respect of Fine Arts, the state has an abounding tradition of both ancient and contemporary art and artists. The traditional Kerala murals are found in ancient temples, churches and palaces across the State. These paintings, mostly dating to between the 9th to 12th centuries AD, display a distinct style, and a colour code which is predominantly ochre and green.

Natural Beauty

Kerala is blessed with lush greenery, pristine beaches, picturesque hill stations, and diverse wildlife. The Western Ghats in Kerala are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and home to rich biodiversity. Most of Kerala, whose native habitat consists of wet evergreen rainforests at lower elevations and highland deciduous and semi-evergreen forests in the east, is subject to a humid tropical climate. However, significant variations in terrain and elevation have resulted in a land whose biodiversity registers as among the world's most significant. Most of Kerala's significantly biodiverse tracts of wilderness lie in the evergreen forests of its easternmost districts.

Cuisine

Kerala cuisine is known for its distinct flavors, characterized by the use of coconut, spices, and seafood. Kerala cuisine offers a multitude of both vegetarian and non-vegetarian dishes prepared using fish, poultry and red meat with rice as a typical accompaniment. Popular dishes include appam with stew, Kerala fish curry, puttu with kadala curry, and banana chips. Rice is the staple food of Kerala, and it is usually served with a variety of side dishes, such as sambar, rasam, and thoran. The use of coconut is also prominent in Kerala cuisine, and it is used in different forms, such as grated, roasted, or ground into a paste.

Tourism and Festivals

Kerala is a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world with its diverse attractions, including backwaters, beaches, hill stations, wildlife sanctuaries, and cultural experiences. The major festival in Kerala is Onam. Kerala has also a number of religious festivals. Thrissur Pooram, Attukal Pongala, Beema Palli Uroos, and Chettikulangara Bharani are the major temple festivals in Kerala. The Thrissur Pooram is conducted at the Vadakumnathan temple, Thrissur. The Sivarathri is also an important festival in Kerala. Aluva Sivarathri festival is the most famous festival related to Sivaratri. Festivals like Christmas and Easter are observed by the Christians.
